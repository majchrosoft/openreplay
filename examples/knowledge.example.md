# Knowledge Base Example

This is a sample knowledge base for a tech YouTube channel about AI and automation.

```markdown
# Knowledge Base

## Tech Channel Knowledge

Q: What kind of content do you create?
A: I create high-quality videos about AI, automation, and software development. My content is designed to help developers learn new skills and stay up-to-date with the latest technologies.

Q: How often do you upload videos?
A: I upload new videos every Tuesday and Friday. Make sure to subscribe and turn on notifications so you don't miss any uploads!

Q: What programming languages do you cover?
A: I primarily cover Python, JavaScript, and TypeScript. I also have videos on Docker, Kubernetes, and cloud platforms like AWS and Azure.

Q: Do you accept feature requests?
A: Yes! I love hearing from my audience. Please leave your suggestions in the comments or join my Discord community.

Q: Are your tutorials beginner-friendly?
A: Absolutely! I start with the basics and build up to more advanced topics. Each video includes clear explanations and step-by-step demonstrations.

## General Information

Q: Is your content free?
A: Yes, all my videos are completely free. However, if you'd like to support me, you can join my Patreon or buy me a coffee!

Q: Do you offer consulting services?
A:Yes, I offer consulting services for companies looking to implement AI solutions. Reach out through my contact page for more information.

Q: Can you cover my specific topic?
A: I try to cover topics that interest my audience most. If you want to see a video on a specific topic, leave a comment or suggest it on Discord!

Q: What equipment do you use?
A: I use a high-end gaming PC, Blue Yeti microphone, and Logitech C920 webcam. I edit with DaVinci Resolve and create graphics with Figma.

## Community Guidelines

Q: What are your community guidelines?
A: I maintain a respectful and inclusive community. Please be kind, no hate speech, and no spam. All comments are moderated to ensure a positive environment.

Q: How do you handle bug reports?
A: If you find a bug in one of my code examples, please open an issue on GitHub. I'll review it and fix it as soon as possible!

Q: Can I use your code in my projects?
A: Yes! All code examples are provided under the MIT license, meaning you can use them freely in personal and commercial projects.
```

## Tips for Creating Knowledge Base

1. **Focus on your content**: Include information specific to your channel or product
2. **Answer common questions**: Add Q&A pairs for frequently asked questions
3. **Be specific**: General responses are less helpful than specific ones
4. **Keep it updated**: Review and update your knowledge base regularly
5. **Use natural language**: Write Q&A pairs as people would actually ask

## Knowledge Base Modes

- `knowledge_preferred`: Use knowledge if available, fallback otherwise (recommended)
- `knowledge_only`: Only use knowledge, no fallback
- `fallback_only`: Skip knowledge, use generic fallback
- `general`: No knowledge base, default responses
